When actions are torques, not buttons

The book ends at PPO, and PPO does handle continuous actions — but the algorithms that own robotics are off-policy actor-critics. This chapter walks the lineage DQN started: DDPG bolts an actor onto Q-learning, TD3 fixes its lies, and SAC rebuilds the objective itself around entropy. Part 1

DDPG — an actor to replace the argmax

Q-learning's whole engine is . With four buttons that's a lookup; with a 7-joint arm the action is a vector in and the max is an optimization problem per training step. DDPG's move: train a second network, a deterministic actor , whose only job is to be the argmax. The critic learns exactly like DQN; the actor climbs the critic's gradient:

Chain rule, nothing more: how the score changes with the action, times how the action changes with the weights. Because the policy is deterministic, exploration must be bolted on — Gaussian noise added to actions at collection time — and because learning is off-policy, DQN's whole toolkit carries over: replay buffer, target networks, but with a gentler update. Instead of hard-copying weights every 10k steps, DDPG polyak-averages them a little every step:

for param, target_param in zip(actor.parameters(), target_actor.parameters()):
    target_param.data.copy_(tau * param.data + (1 - tau) * target_param.data)  # tau = 0.005
Analogy

The critic is a terrain map of action-quality; the actor is a hiker standing on it. Rather than surveying the whole map for the summit (argmax), the hiker just walks uphill from where they stand — fast, but they'll happily climb a hill that the map drew wrong.

In the wild

DDPG (2015) was the first deep-RL algorithm to make simulated locomotion and manipulation work from raw states — the proof that DQN's ideas survived the jump to . It's rarely deployed today, but its skeleton lives inside everything in this chapter.

Part 2

TD3 — the critic is a flatterer

DDPG is famously brittle, and the root cause is overestimation bias: the actor is explicitly trained to find actions where Q is large — including where Q is large by error. The actor exploits the critic's noise, the target values inherit the inflation, and the run collapses. TD3 (Twin Delayed DDPG) is three surgical fixes:

Clipped double-Q: train two critics; targets use the pessimist — . Two independent errors rarely inflate the same action.

Delayed policy updates: update the actor once per two critic updates — let the map settle before the hiker moves.

Target policy smoothing: add clipped noise to the target action, so the target value is an average over a small neighborhood — Q can't hide a narrow phantom spike where the actor would find it.

Rhyme check

You met this disease in Chapter 4: the max in Q-learning's target chases noise upward, and Double DQN decoupled selection from evaluation to fight it. TD3's twin critics are the same instinct wearing continuous-control clothes. Overestimation is not a DQN quirk — it is what maximization does to noise, anywhere.

Part 3

SAC — put exploration in the objective

TD3 patched DDPG's symptoms; SAC (Soft Actor-Critic) rewrites the goal. Maximize reward and stay as random as you can afford to be:

The entropy term is Chapter 6's entropy bonus promoted from regularizer to first-class citizen of the objective. The actor is stochastic again — a squashed Gaussian, with — and that reparameterization is what lets gradients flow through sampling: the randomness enters as an external input, so the sample is a differentiable function of θ. The temperature α is even tuned automatically, by gradient descent against a target entropy. Off-policy replay from TD3, twin critics from TD3, but the exploration problem dissolves: the policy explores because the objective pays it to.

# CleanRL sac_continuous_action.py — the soft target and the actor loss
next_q = torch.min(qf1_target(s2, a2), qf2_target(s2, a2)) - alpha * next_logprob
y = r + gamma * (1 - d) * next_q                      # entropy inside the backup
actor_loss = (alpha * logprob - torch.min(qf1(s, a), qf2(s, a))).mean()
Analogy

A chef paid for good dishes and for keeping the menu varied. Early on, variety pays for itself in discoveries; as the kitchen masters the classics, α shrinks and the menu narrows toward the hits — but never to a single dish.

In the wild — real robots

SAC's sample efficiency (replay!) and stability made it the default for learning on physical hardware — quadruped locomotion learned in ~20 minutes of real-world experience, dexterous manipulation, chip-floorplanning variants. Where every trial costs real time and real wear, off-policy wins.

Choosing your fighter

PPO when rollouts are cheap and parallel (simulators, games, RLHF); SAC when samples are precious (hardware, slow sims). PPO squeezes a fresh batch; SAC remembers every batch it ever saw. Run Project 5 to feel the difference — then break SAC's α on purpose.
